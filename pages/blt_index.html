Blinky Light Thing:

(that's the technical term)


It's hard to know what to trust these days and it going to get harder.

Being able to distinguish between and authentic and generative is already a blurred line.

Thankfully deep fakes are still in the realm of "video production" meaning that they are generated and then released to the public.

Fake live streaming with real-time filters is around the corner.

This means that the person on your favorite streaming live cast might not be who you think they are...

or the colleague in your zoom meeting is an imposter...

or the government official making a public address is not just a political puppet, but someone else entirely!

What makes a live stream authentic anyway?

For broadcast media we rely on the producers and reporters to display legitimate content.

For narrowcast media we rely on the reputation of the presenter

This means we need to trust in two things:

1) *Identity Attestation*: The producer or presenter is who they claim to be and is willing to put their reputation on the line.

2) *Contemporaneousness*: The content being shared as "live" is in fact happening now (or at least within a few seconds of lag time)
